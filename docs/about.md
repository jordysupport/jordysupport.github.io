# About Jordy Support

Jordy Support exists to close the gap between seeing an AI demo and building something you can understand, supervise, troubleshoot, and trust.

## The mission

Provide free, practical guidance that helps people:

- choose a sensible AI setup;
- turn vague ideas into bounded tasks;
- give agents useful context and durable instructions;
- connect tools with safer permissions;
- design automations with validation and failure handling;
- recover when terminals, Git, connectors, or workflows break;
- create useful systems without pretending uncertainty has disappeared.

## Editorial principles

### Practical before impressive

A small draft workflow with clear checks is better than a complicated autonomous demo nobody can operate.

### Beginner-friendly without hiding risk

Instructions explain what a command or permission changes. “Copy and paste this” is not enough when the action could alter a system.

### Sources before confidence

Important factual claims should be traceable. Missing information should remain missing rather than becoming plausible filler.

### Least privilege

Read-only, limited folders, test accounts, drafts, and approval gates are normal design choices—not signs of failure.

### Recovery matters

Good guidance includes previews, diffs, logs, backups, version history, failed-item handling, and ways to undo changes.

### AI where it helps

Deterministic rules should handle exact validation, calculations, and hard policy when possible. AI should be used for interpretation and generation where its flexibility creates value.

## Community roots

The project grew from answering recurring questions for people learning AI through several online communities. Jared Rhod's work is acknowledged and linked through [his official website](https://jaredrhod.com/) as one community reference. Jordy Support is independent, does not reproduce his material, and is not his official support site.

## Not professional advice

The site provides general educational information. It is not legal, medical, financial, cybersecurity, employment, or compliance advice. High-impact decisions need the responsible qualified person and the policies that apply to the situation.

## Keeping the site useful

A good contribution:

- solves a real recurring problem;
- includes the exact symptom or use case;
- gives safe diagnostic steps in a clear order;
- distinguishes facts from assumptions;
- links current official documentation for product-specific behavior;
- explains expected results and recovery;
- avoids secrets and private data;
- remains understandable to a motivated beginner.

You can contribute through the [GitHub repository](https://github.com/jordysupport/jordysupport.github.io).
