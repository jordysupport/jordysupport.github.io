# Prompting that works

A [**prompt**](../resources/glossary.md#prompt) is just your request. Good ones aren't clever — they're clear. Five ingredients cover 95% of it.

## The five ingredients

1. **The job.** What you want, in one sentence.
2. **The material.** Paste or attach what it should work from.
3. **The audience.** Who this is for.
4. **The shape.** How you want the answer: length, format, tone.
5. **The rules.** What it must and must not do.

You don't need all five every time. But when an answer disappoints, one of these was missing — usually the shape or the material.

## Before and after

**Vague:** "Write something about our new service."

**Clear:** "Write a 150-word announcement of our new lawn-care package for our existing customers. Friendly, no hype words. Use only the details below: [paste details]. End with 'Reply to book.'"

Same AI. The second one wins every time.

## Two habits that beat any trick

- **Show an example.** "Here's one I liked — match this style" outperforms a paragraph of style adjectives.
- **Let it interview you.** Can't articulate what you want? Say: *"Ask me questions one at a time until you have what you need, then do the job."* This is exactly how the [playbooks](../playbooks/index.md) work.
