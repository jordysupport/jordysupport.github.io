Get a short, honest answer to a real question — with sources you can click — instead of a confident guess.

## Option 1 — Install it once (recommended)

[Download the research playbook](../downloads/research-brief-skill.zip){ .md-button .md-button--primary }

Give the zip to your AI and send:

```text title="Copy this message"
Unzip this file and follow the instructions in INSTALL.md.
```

Your AI saves the playbook to your notes. From then on, just say:

```text title="Your start phrase, forever"
Start my research playbook
```

**What happens next:** it asks you five short questions — what you want to know, why, who'll read it, what it must cover, how current it needs to be. Then it researches, shows its sources, and reviews the result with you.

## Option 2 — Quick copy-paste (any AI chat)

```text title="Copy this prompt"
Interview me one question at a time to build a research brief:
what I want to find out, what decision it helps with, who will
read it, 3-5 things it must cover, and how current it must be.
Then research it. Rules: cite sources for important claims,
separate facts from your own reasoning, say what you don't know,
and never invent a source. End with: summary, key findings,
risks and unknowns, what it means for my decision, source list.
```

## Before you trust it

- [ ] Click the sources behind anything you'll act on.
- [ ] Check that dates are shown where they matter.
- [ ] Make sure "we don't know" is stated, not papered over.
- [ ] Confirm the conclusion answers *your* question.

!!! warning "A polished answer is not a checked answer"
    AI can sound certain and be wrong. Open the two or three sources that matter most.

## You're done when

Another person could read your brief and click their way to the evidence behind it.
